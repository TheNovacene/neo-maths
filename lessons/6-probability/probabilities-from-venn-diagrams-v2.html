Focus Concept: The Addition Rule for Probability

The probability of either event A or event B happening is given by the addition rule:

P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)

Here, the universal set is a full deck of 52 playing cards.

  • Set A = All Kings
  • Set B = All Black Cards

🎯 Your task is to use the interactive Venn diagram to sort all cards correctly, then use your diagram to calculate probabilities based on what you’ve placed.

💬 What happens if you forget to subtract the overlap? What does the intersection represent in this context?

🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

Probability helps us interpret uncertain situations — but only if we understand how events might overlap.

For example, in public health data, one person may belong to more than one group (e.g., "under 18" and "at risk").

✊ Misinterpreting overlaps can lead to double-counting, flawed policies, or unfair decisions.

🧵 Where in your life might groups overlap? Why is it important to notice the intersection?

Use the interactive game above:

  1. Click “Deal Card” to reveal a card from the 52-card deck.
  2. Drag and drop the card into the correct region of the Venn diagram:
    • A only = King but not black
    • B only = Black but not King
    • A ∩ B = Black Kings
    • Outside = Neither King nor black
  3. After all cards are placed, answer the probability questions:
    • P(King)
    • P(Black)
    • P(Black ∩ King)
    • P(Black ∪ King)
  4. Click “Check Answers” to see feedback.
  5. Use the “Try Another” button to explore new combinations (e.g., Queens and Red Cards).
