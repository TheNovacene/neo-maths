Focus Concept: Using Tree Diagrams to Represent Independent and Conditional Probability

Tree diagrams help us map all possible outcomes of multi-stage events and calculate the probabilities of combinations.

Key Ideas:

  • Independent events: The outcome of one stage does not affect the next (e.g., flipping two coins).
  • Conditional events: The outcome of one stage influences the probabilities in the next stage (e.g., drawing counters without replacement).

Use this interactive tool to build your own tree diagrams and explore how probabilities multiply along each branch.

🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

We use probability trees to plan for uncertainty — from medical testing to climate forecasting, from risk analysis to AI modelling.

⚖️ But we must be careful — assumptions of independence in real-life scenarios can lead to mistakes or biased decisions.

Use the interactive app above:

  1. Select a context: Counters or Custom Event.
  2. Choose “with replacement” or “without replacement” to explore independence vs conditionality.
  3. Build your tree diagram using the visual editor.
  4. Click on different branches to see total outcome probabilities.
  5. Try calculating:
    • The probability of a specific outcome (e.g., “Red then Green”).
    • The total probability of all paths ending with a specific result.
