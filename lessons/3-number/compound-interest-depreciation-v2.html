Compound Interest & Depreciation

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 40–60 mins | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

Explore how money grows or shrinks over time through compound interest and depreciation.

Key ideas

  • Compound interest grows money exponentially using repeated multiplication.
  • Depreciation reduces value over time, often at a constant percentage rate.

Formulas

Compound Interest:
A = P(1 + r/n)nt

  • A = Final amount
  • P = Principal (initial amount)
  • r = Annual interest rate (as a decimal)
  • n = Number of times interest is compounded per year
  • t = Time in years

Depreciation:
V = V₀(1 - d)t

  • V = Final value
  • V₀ = Initial value
  • d = Depreciation rate (as a decimal)
  • t = Time in years

Quick check

  • If £500 grows at 4% compounded annually for 3 years, what is the final amount?
  • If a laptop worth £1200 depreciates at 15% each year, what is it worth after 2 years?
