The Cosine Rule — Practice Companion
Pure 1, Trigonometry · Lesson 2

Eight exam-style questions on the cosine rule (both side-finding SAS and angle-finding SSS). Whenever you're stuck, you have four kinds of help, in this order:

🤔 Socratic prompt — a question to get you thinking.   💡 Hint — a piece of method.   ✅ Check my answer — try as many times as you like.   👀 Worked solution — the full method, unlocked once you've genuinely tried.

Give angles in degrees to 1 decimal place and lengths to 2 decimal places unless told otherwise. Calculator must be in degrees mode.

💛 Reflection

Before you close this, take a moment:

  • When the cosine rule's correction term came out negative, did you anchor that back to "C must be obtuse" — or just compute and trust? Both are fine, but the anchor is faster.
  • Which feels more confident now — picking when to use the sine rule, or picking when to use the cosine rule? Whichever feels less confident is what to practise more.
  • If you taught one of these questions to a friend, which would you choose, and why?
