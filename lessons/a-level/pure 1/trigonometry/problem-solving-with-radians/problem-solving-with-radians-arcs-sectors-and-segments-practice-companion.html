Problem Solving with Radians, Arcs, Sectors & Segments
Pure 1, Trigonometry · Lesson 5 (synthesis)

Eight exam-style questions that mix arc length s = rθ, sector area ½r²θ, and the segment ½r²(θ − sin θ) — the real skill here is choosing and combining the right tools. Whenever you are stuck, you have four kinds of help, in this order:

🤔 Socratic prompt — a question to get you thinking.   💡 Hint — a piece of method.   ✅ Check my answer — try as many times as you like.   👀 Worked solution — the full method, unlocked once you have genuinely tried.

Use radians unless told otherwise, and keep your calculator in radian mode. Give answers to the accuracy each question states, keeping full accuracy in your working and rounding only at the end. For a segment, remember it is sector − triangle.

💛 Reflection

Before you close this, take a moment:

  • Did any answer come out the wrong size? Your anchors are π ≈ 3.14 (a half-turn) and π/2 ≈ 1.57 (a right angle). Sense-checking an angle against those catches most slips.
  • How many times did a wrong answer turn out to be the calculator in the wrong mode rather than a real error? Noticing that pattern is how you stop it happening in the exam.
  • Which felt harder — recovering θ from the information given, or the sector − triangle subtraction for the segment? Whichever it was tells you exactly what to practise next.
  • For the segment-perimeter question, did you remember it is the curved arc (rθ) plus the straight chord (2r sin(θ/2)), not just one of them? That single habit earns marks.
