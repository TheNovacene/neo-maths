Simultaneous Equations

Practice Companion · Pure Mathematics 1 · Algebra and Functions

Eight questions, building from straightforward to exam-style. Each one comes with a four-tier support ladder — use it in order, and only as far as you need:

🤔 Socratic prompt asks you a question to get you thinking · 💡 Hint gives you a piece of method · ✅ Check my answer tells you if you're right and nudges you if not · 👀 Worked solution is the last resort, and unlocks once you've genuinely tried. The aim isn't to avoid the ladder — it's to climb only as high as you need, and to notice where you needed it.

💛 Before you finish — a reflection

Look back over the eight questions and notice, without judgement:

  • Which case felt more secure — linear-linear or linear-quadratic? Starting from your stronger case is a sensible strategy when you're tired.
  • On the questions where you used the discriminant, did "how many roots" and "how many intersection points" feel like the same question yet? If not, that's the connection worth revisiting.
  • Where did you reach for the ladder, and which rung actually helped? A Socratic nudge, a hint, or checking-and-revising? Knowing which kind of help unsticks you is a tool for every future topic.

There's no score that matters here more than that last noticing.
