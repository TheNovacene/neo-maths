Inequalities

Practice Companion · Pure Mathematics 1 · Algebra and Functions

Eight questions, building from straightforward to exam-style. Each comes with a four-tier support ladder — use it in order, and only as far as you need:

🤔 Socratic prompt asks you a question to get you thinking · 💡 Hint gives you a piece of method · ✅ Check my answer tells you if you're right and nudges you if not · 👀 Worked solution is the last resort, and unlocks once you've genuinely tried. Two habits to carry through: pause at every negative divide, and for quadratics, sketch the parabola and read the region.

💛 Before you finish — a reflection

Look back over the eight questions and notice, without judgement:

  • On the sign-flip questions, did you catch the flip as you divided, or only when checking? Building the pause-at-every-negative-divide habit is worth more than speed.
  • For the quadratics, did sketching the parabola make "outside the roots" versus "between the roots" feel obvious rather than memorised? If so, trust the picture — it rebuilds itself when a rule won't.
  • Which rung of the ladder actually unstuck you when you needed it? Knowing whether a question, a hint, or checking-and-revising helps you most is a tool for every topic to come.

That last noticing matters more than any score.
