🔷 Exploring Symmetry: Reflection & Rotation

Use the following interactive tools to investigate both line (reflective) symmetry and rotational symmetry in a square. Explore how shapes map onto themselves under these transformations.

🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

What patterns or regularities can you find in how squares behave under reflection and rotation? Can you describe the fixed points or invariant features?

🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

Why do humans care about symmetry? How does symmetry appear in human culture, design, or architecture?

🎨 Creativity — How can you make, imagine, or express this in your own way?

Can you use symmetry creatively? Try designing a symmetric shape or pattern that reflects your own style or interests.

🔄 Movement — How does this idea move — and how can you put it to work?

How do designers and engineers use symmetry when building things — from logos to machines? Can you find an example where symmetry improves efficiency or beauty?

🌙 Rest — Where’s the space to breathe, pace yourself, and let it settle?

Explore how symmetry appears in biology — in body shapes, flowers, or other living systems. Why might this be important for survival?

🌱 Nutrition · Final Task — What real-world need does this feed?

Summarise what you’ve discovered about symmetry. Can you describe how a square maps onto itself through both rotation and reflection? Can you apply these ideas to other shapes?
