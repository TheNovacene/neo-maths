✨ Exploring Bearings: Navigating with Angles

Use the interactive compass tool below to investigate how bearings are measured and applied in navigation.

Copy of Interactive Compass Bearing Web App Design by Gerry Docherty
🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

Learn how bearings are measured clockwise from the North direction and expressed in three digits, such as 045° or 270°. Bearings are commonly used in navigation, surveying, and direction-finding.

🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

Consider how ancient civilizations and explorers used stars and basic tools to measure directions. Bearings helped ships navigate oceans and traders cross deserts, linking people across vast distances.

🎨 Creativity — How can you make, imagine, or express this in your own way?

Reflect on how knowing how to read a compass can enhance your independence and confidence in the outdoors, from hiking trails to orienteering challenges.

🔄 Movement — How does this idea move — and how can you put it to work?

Bearings are used in architecture, engineering, aviation, and mobile navigation apps. Explore how modern GPS technology still relies on principles of angular measurement.

🌙 Rest — Where’s the space to breathe, pace yourself, and let it settle?

Understanding direction and orientation activates spatial reasoning areas of the brain. Try mentally rotating the compass in different scenarios to strengthen your cognitive mapping skills.

🌱 Nutrition · Final Task — What real-world need does this feed?

Click “Set Target” in the app above. Rotate the arrow to match the target bearing. Record at least 3 successful matches and describe any strategies you used. Why is a small margin of error (e.g., ±5°) acceptable in some real-world applications?
