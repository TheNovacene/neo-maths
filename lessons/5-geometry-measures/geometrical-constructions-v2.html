Geometrical Constructions: Bisectors

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 40–60 mins | Inquiry-Based | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

Constructions in geometry use only a compass and straightedge—no measuring. We can create precise, logical diagrams using basic tools and ancient rules.

Key Concepts

  • Perpendicular Bisector: A line dividing another line into two equal parts at 90°.
  • Angle Bisector: A line that splits an angle into two equal angles.
  • These constructions rely on the properties of circles and symmetry.

Quick Check

  • Why do the arcs intersect above and below the line when creating a perpendicular bisector?
  • What do you notice about the point where two angle bisectors meet in a triangle?
🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

Builders in ancient Egypt and Greece relied on geometric constructions to build straight roads, walls, and temples. Without digital tools, they used string, sticks, and chalk to achieve precision. Why do you think this method of construction has endured for thousands of years?

2️⃣ Constructing the Angle Bisector

Place the compass on the angle’s vertex and draw an arc. Then use the intersection points to create overlapping arcs. Connect the vertex to where the arcs intersect.
