Mathematical Proof

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 40–60 mins | Inquiry-Based | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

Proof in mathematics is a logical argument that shows a statement must always be true.

Key Concepts:

  • Definitions: e.g., even numbers are of the form \(2k\), odd numbers are \(2k+1\)
  • Algebraic Proof: Using algebra to generalise a pattern or property
  • Contradiction: Assuming the opposite leads to a false statement
  • Geometric Proof: Using diagrams, angles, and congruence

Examples:

  • Prove the sum of two odd numbers is even
  • Prove \(n^2 - n\) is always even for integer \(n\)
  • Prove the sum of the angles in a triangle is 180°
🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

From Euclid’s ancient geometry to Andrew Wiles' proof of Fermat’s Last Theorem, mathematical proof has shaped our understanding of truth and logic. Why do you think mathematics relies so heavily on proof rather than opinion?

🎨 Creativity — How can you make, imagine, or express this in your own way?
  • Write your own explanation of a proof in words only—no algebra!
  • Create a comic strip showing someone discovering a counterexample.
  • Record a voice note explaining why proof matters in maths and in life.
🔄 Movement — How does this idea move — and how can you put it to work?

Explore and build different proofs using this interactive whiteboard tool. Choose a statement and try to construct the logical steps required to prove it true.

Try This:
- What strategy do you use when approaching a new proof?
- Can you spot where an incorrect “proof” goes wrong?
- What happens if you try proving a statement using different methods (algebraic vs geometric)?

🌙 Rest — Where’s the space to breathe, pace yourself, and let it settle?

Act out a mathematical proof with a partner—assign roles like "assumption", "deduction", and "conclusion". Move as each step is ‘performed’ aloud.

🌱 Nutrition · Final Task — What real-world need does this feed?

Task: Choose one of the proof statements and construct your own clear and logical argument. Present it using words, algebra, and visuals.

  • Clarity & Logic: Each step should follow from the last.
  • Precision: Use algebra or definitions accurately.
  • Reflection: Can you think of any counterexamples?
