Rearranging Formulae

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 40–60 mins | Inquiry-Based | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

Rearranging a formula means changing its subject—isolating a chosen variable on one side of the equation.

Key ideas

  • We use inverse operations to undo what's been done to the subject.
  • Treat both sides equally—whatever you do to one side, do to the other.
  • Use logical steps: isolate, undo operations, simplify.
  • Start simple: \( A = bh \) → rearranged for \( h \): \( h = \frac{A}{b} \)
  • Try moderate examples like \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)

Quick check

  • Rearrange \( v = u + at \) to make \( t \) the subject.
  • Make \( r \) the subject in \( V = \pi r^2 h \)
🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

From calculating interest rates to finding height from volume, rearranging formulae empowers us to solve real problems. It’s like solving a puzzle—you already have the answer, now you just need the right path to the unknown.

  • Design a character who rearranges formulas to solve mysteries!
  • Use colour coding to track steps in your rearrangement.

🌱 Nutrition · Final Task — What real-world need does this feed?

Task: Create your own real-world formula problem and rearrange it in at least two different ways.

  • Conceptual Understanding: Explain each step of your rearrangement.
  • Application: Use your new formula in a practical context (e.g., speed, volume, finance).
  • Independence & Reflection: Reflect on what made certain formulas harder to rearrange than others.
