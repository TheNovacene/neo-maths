Solving Inequalities in Two Variables Using Graphs

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 40–60 mins | Inquiry-Based | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

Graphs can represent not only equations, but also inequalities. Shading the correct region helps us find all the possible solutions that satisfy a condition.

Key Concepts

  • Linear inequalities like y ≤ 2x + 1 describe a region of the coordinate plane.
  • A solid line means points on the line are included (≤ or ≥); a dashed line means they’re not (< or >).
  • The correct side of the line is shaded, representing all the solutions.
  • Systems of inequalities are solved by finding where the shaded regions overlap.

Quick Check

  • What does the inequality y > x look like on a graph?
  • What region satisfies both y ≤ -x + 3 and y ≥ 1?
🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

From zoning areas on maps to defining safe conditions in engineering systems, inequalities help describe limits and boundaries. Where in the real world do you see systems with multiple conditions working at once?

🎨 Creativity — How can you make, imagine, or express this in your own way?
  • Create a coloured diagram showing several inequality regions and where they intersect.
  • Use shading and lines to explain what each region means.
  • Write a reflection: “Solving inequalities graphically helps me see...”
🔄 Movement — How does this idea move — and how can you put it to work?

Use the interactive whiteboard tool below to explore how graphing inequalities works. Try out different inequalities and combine two or more to find solution sets.

🔔 Tip: Use the test-point feature to verify shaded regions. Try graphing systems like y < 2x + 1 and x ≥ 0.

🌙 Rest — Where’s the space to breathe, pace yourself, and let it settle?

Use masking tape on the floor to create coordinate axes. Learners can “walk” into solution regions as inequalities are read out. This helps turn abstract constraints into spatial intuition!

🌱 Nutrition · Final Task — What real-world need does this feed?

Task: Choose a system of two inequalities. Graph them and highlight the solution region. Annotate each boundary and explain your steps.

  • Understanding: Explain the role of dashed vs. solid lines.
  • Accuracy: Plot boundaries and shading correctly.
  • Application: Use real-world contexts like budgeting or logistics.
  • Reflection: What did you find tricky? How did graphing help?
