⚖️ Solving Linear Equations: Balancing Both Sides

GCSE Maths | Foundation & Higher | Key Stage 4 | 40 minutes

🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

"To understand the laws that guide, the patterns hidden deep inside."

In algebra, an equation is like a balance scale — both sides must weigh the same. To solve an equation, we use operations to isolate the variable (usually x) while keeping the equation balanced.

📘 Glossary:

  • Equation: A statement showing two expressions are equal
  • Variable: A letter (like x or y) that stands for a number
  • Inverse Operation: The opposite mathematical operation (e.g. subtraction is inverse of addition)
  • Isolate the Variable: Get the letter by itself on one side of the equation

🔍 Patterns to Know:

  • Start by undoing addition or subtraction
  • Then undo multiplication or division
  • Always do the same thing to both sides
