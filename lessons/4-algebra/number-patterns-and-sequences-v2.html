Number Patterns & Sequences

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel | 40–60 mins | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

Mathematics is full of patterns. Recognising and describing them is one of our oldest and most powerful ways of making sense of the world.

Key Concepts

  • Arithmetic sequences: Increase/decrease by a constant value (e.g. 3, 7, 11, 15...)
  • Geometric sequences: Multiply/divide by a constant value (e.g. 2, 4, 8, 16...)
  • nth-term rule: Describes a general term in a sequence (e.g. \(a_n = 3n + 1\))

Try These

  • What comes next: 5, 10, 15, ___?
  • Find the next three terms: 81, 27, 9, ___?
  • What’s the nth-term of: 2, 5, 8, 11...?
🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

Humans have always noticed patterns — in nature, architecture, music, and time itself. Ancient mathematicians from Babylon to India explored sequences long before calculators. Why do we notice patterns so naturally?

🎨 Creativity — How can you make, imagine, or express this in your own way?
  • Create an abstract artwork using number patterns — colour by multiples or powers.
  • Write a short story where a hidden pattern helps solve a mystery.
  • Make a sequence playlist: each song must follow a pattern in duration or title numbers!

🔍 Prompts for inquiry:
– How do you know if a sequence is arithmetic or geometric?
– Can you find a pattern that is neither?
– What happens when you reverse or alternate a pattern?
– Which sequences grow faster and why?

🌙 Rest — Where’s the space to breathe, pace yourself, and let it settle?

Try clapping, stepping, or drumming a pattern out loud. Alternate fast and slow beats — can your partner guess the rule?

🌱 Nutrition · Final Task — What real-world need does this feed?

Task: Design your own “Mystery Pattern” and challenge a classmate to continue it and describe the rule.
