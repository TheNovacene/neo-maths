Expanding Brackets

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 30–45 mins | Inquiry-Based | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🔎 Reflection — What patterns can you notice — and what do you notice about your own thinking?

Expanding brackets means removing the brackets by multiplying the terms inside with the term(s) outside using the distributive law.

Key Ideas

  • Single Bracket: Distribute the outer term to each term inside: a(b + c) = ab + ac
  • Double Bracket: Multiply each term in the first bracket by each term in the second:
  • (x + a)(x + b) = x^2 + (a + b)x + ab
  • Negative numbers and powers require careful attention to signs and exponents.

Quick Check

  • Expand: 2(x + 3)
  • Expand: (x + 5)(x - 2)
  • Expand and simplify: -3(x - 4)
🤝 Connection — Whose story does this maths tell, and who does it connect?

Distributive thinking has been used in areas like architecture and textile patterning. Multiplying across parts and combining outcomes mirrors how humans divide and conquer complex tasks. Can you think of any other real-world examples where things are “unpacked” like brackets?

🎨 Creativity — How can you make, imagine, or express this in your own way?
  • Use colour to visualise each multiplication step with arrows.
  • Create a graphic or animation to explain the FOIL process.
  • Write a story or comic strip that personifies brackets being "expanded" into individual terms.
